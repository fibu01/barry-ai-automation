## TIP #4
STATUS: Ready

TITLE: Teach AI to Sound Like You
SUBTITLE: Stop editing every AI draft. Teach it your voice once.
TAGLINE: When AI knows how you write, every draft it creates feels like yours.
RETHINKING TITLE: Rethinking Your Writing Voice
RETHINKING BODY: AI drafts often feel off because the tool doesn't know how you communicate. The fix is simple. Show it a few examples of your own writing and ask it to match your tone. After that, you spend a lot less time editing and a lot more time sending.
HOW TO TRY IT INTRO: Paste two or three of your own emails into Claude, Copilot, or Gemini and try one of these:
BULLET 1: "Here are three emails I've written. Use these as my style guide, then help me draft a message to my team about our upcoming deadline."
BULLET 2: "I write in a direct, friendly tone. Keep it short and skip the formal language. Now help me respond to this student inquiry."
BULLET 3: "Match my voice based on these writing samples, then draft a follow-up email to my department chair about the budget request."
FOLLOW UP LINE: Save your best examples in a document so you can paste them in any time you need a draft that sounds like you.
WEEKLY CHALLENGE: Find two emails you've sent recently that sound like you at your best. Paste them into your AI tool and say: "This is how I write. Use this as your style guide." Then ask it to draft something you actually need right now. Notice how much less editing the result needs.
GET STARTED: "Here are a few examples of how I write. Use these as your style guide and help me draft a message about [your task]."
